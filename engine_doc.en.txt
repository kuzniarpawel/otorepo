This document describes the public and internal helper API of the engine and the adopted
conventions. Keep it updated as the engine evolves.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 CONVENTIONS AND COORDINATE SYSTEMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Head frame:  x = right, y = up (skull), z = forward (nose).
World: "up" axis = +y; world gravity = (0,-1,0).
Sides (ear/side): "L" = left ear, "P" = right ear.
Canals (canal): "posterior", "horizontal", "anterior".

Quaternion: q = [w, x, y, z]; rotates HEAD vectors ‚Üí WORLD
            (v_world = q ‚äó v_head ‚äó q*).

Nystagmus components (anatomical, patient frame), returned as {h, v, t}:
   h: + to the right / ‚àí to the left        (horizontal)
   v: + upward / ‚àí downward         (vertical)
   t: + upper poles to the right / ‚àí to the left  (torsional)

Ewald's laws used in the engine:
   I   ‚Äî plane specificity: the fast-phase axis of a given canal (quickPhase)
   II  ‚Äî horizontal canal: AMPULLOPETAL flow is excitatory; excitation > inhibition
   III ‚Äî vertical canals: AMPULLOFUGAL flow is excitatory

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PUBLIC API (returned from the module)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

‚Äî STAGE 0 (rule-based) ‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî

isExcitatory(canal, flow) -> bool
   Whether the given flow direction excites the canal (Ewald's law II/III).
   flow: "ampullopetal" | "ampullofugal".
   Returns: horizontal ‚Üí (flow==="ampullopetal"); vertical ‚Üí (flow==="ampullofugal").

quickPhase(canal, ear) -> {h, v, t}
   Fast-phase axis for canal EXCITATION, ipsiversive to the ear (Ewald I).
   ear: "L"|"P". (ipsi = ear==="P" ? +1 : -1)
   horizontal ‚Üí {h:ipsi, v:0,    t:0}      (horizontal toward the excited ear; torsion masked)
   posterior  ‚Üí {h:0,    v:+0.93,t:ipsi}   (upbeat + torsion toward the ear; real v:t‚âà0.93:1)
   anterior   ‚Üí {h:0,    v:-1,   t:0}      (PURE downbeat; geometric torsion ~0.78 MASKED ‚Äî NYS_SHOW.anterior.t=false)
   NOTE ‚Äî CLINICAL MASK (NYS_SHOW): quickPhase returns the UI convention for BPPV (anterior = pure downbeat,
   horizontal without torsion). The FULL geometry (anterior torsion PRESERVED, for lateralization) ‚Üí NeuroVOR.qpFull()
   / Vestibular.nysMag(); used by the HINTS layer (vHIT saccade / verticalBeat / SCDS). See the "CLINICAL MASK" section.

nysMag(canal, ear) -> {h, v, t}
   Full geometric proportions of the nystagmus from REAL canal normals (CANAL_NORMALS), normalized
   to max=1. Does NOT apply the NYS_SHOW clinical mask (anterior-canal torsion PRESERVED) ‚Äî cf. quickPhase
   (same geometry, masked to the BPPV convention). Source of the full geometry for NeuroVOR
   (qpFull ‚Üí vHIT saccade / verticalBeat / pressureStimulus-SCDS). ear: "L"|"P".

nystagmus({canal, ear, excited=true}) -> {h, v, t}
   Fast phase for the given, excited/inhibited canal.
   Inhibition (excited=false) reverses the sign of all components.
   Future use: unilateral loss (excited:false), push-pull.

‚Äî STAGE 1 (static geometry) ‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî

gHead(q) -> [x, y, z]
   The gravity vector expressed in the head frame for orientation q.
   Upright-standing head (q=[1,0,0,0]) ‚Üí [0,-1,0].

qSupineYaw(deg) -> q
   "Supine head-hanging" orientation (a -100¬∞ pitch about the interaural axis)
   with a head yaw of `deg` about the skull axis (y). Use: Dix-Hallpike (¬±45),
   Roll (¬±90). Note: positive deg = right ear down (3D convention, NOT the UI schematic).

qPitch(deg) -> q
   Pure pitch about the interaural axis (x). +deg = bow forward (bow),
   -deg = lean back (lean). Use: Bow & Lean test.

position({canal, side, variant, q}) -> {excited, mag, h, v, t}
   Static stimulation of the AFFECTED canal in head orientation q.
   side: "L"|"P"; variant: "canalo" (geotropic) | "cupulo" (apogeotropic).
   Mechanism: projection of gravity onto the canal's excitation axis ‚Üí flow ‚Üí Ewald.
     - horizontal:  proj = g ¬∑ uHC(side);  excited = (variant==="cupulo" ? proj<0 : proj>0)
                 (Ewald II: geo/apo reverse)
     - vertical:  proj = g ¬∑ GEXC[canal];  excited = proj>0
                 (Ewald III; no geo/apo reversal for vertical canals)
   excited: bool; mag = |proj| (raw force); {h,v,t} = quickPhase with the sign of excitation.
   VALIDATED: Dix-Hallpike (posterior), Roll (geo/apo), Bow & Lean.

‚Äî STAGE 2 (particle dynamics) ‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî‚Äî

simulateCanalith({canal, side, timeline, q0=null, dt=0.05, tauP=6.5, tauC=5, gc=1.6, phiExit=178, fStat=0.04, adh=0.2, size="medium", rep=0, fatTau=2.0, fatFloor=0.06, crusArc=12, crusGrav=0.6, crusFling=145}) -> [{t, xi, phi, exited}, ...]
   Simulation of CANALITHIASIS: the otolith as a particle on the canal arc.
   [PHENOMENOLOGICAL MODEL ‚Äî a proxy for the dynamics, not full hydrodynamics; see the "MODEL NATURE" header
    and "SIMPLIFICATIONS RELATIVE TO FULL MODELS".]
   timeline: array of segments [{q, tTrans, tHold}, ...]
       q      ‚Äî target orientation (quaternion)
       tTrans ‚Äî transition time to q [s] (slerp interpolation from the previous q)
       tHold  ‚Äî hold time at q [s]
   q0 ‚Äî STARTING orientation (quaternion); default null = the q of the FIRST segment (backward compatibility).
       NOTE (semantics of the 1st segment): with q0=null the first segment does NOT interpolate from the neutral position ‚Äî
       its tTrans is time ALREADY IN the starting position, NOT a transition from sitting. To force a transition from a given
       orientation to the 1st q, pass q0 (e.g. [1,0,0,0] = neutral head). UI convention: the 1st segment has tTrans:0,
       and the transition is realized by the NEXT segment (maneuverTimeline: tTrans:0.8 per step). Analogously for simulateCupulolith.
   Model:
       œÜ ‚Äî arc angle of the particle (ampulla œÜ=0, exit/common crus œÜ‚âà180¬∞); lower bound 3¬∞.
       Overdamped motion (Stokes drag, no inertia):
           dœÜ/dt = (g_head ¬∑ tangent(œÜ)) / tauP
       Cupula (first-order model, long time constant):
           dŒæ/dt = -Œæ/tauC + gc ¬∑ exc ¬∑ dœÜ/dt   (exc: +1 vertical / -1 horizontal)
   OTOLITH ADHESION (latency):
       The otolith is initially attached; the bond (adh) is broken by a sustained tangential force
       above the threshold fStat ‚Üí a delayed start = LATENCY. A stronger provocation = shorter
       latency (consistent with the clinic). Once broken, the otolith no longer re-attaches in this session.
   COMMON-CRUS CHAMBER + EXPULSION INTO THE UTRICLE (VERTICAL canals: posterior+anterior; the horizontal has no crus):
       Anatomically, "reaching the end of the arc" (œÜ=phiExit) ‚âÝ "falling into the utricle" ‚Äî these are TWO events.
       ‚Ä¢ HORIZONTAL CANAL: no common crus ‚Üí the non-ampullary end opens DIRECTLY into the utricle: after œÜ‚â•phiExit
         the otolith enters the utricle ONE-WAY (exited). (Unchanged from the previous version.)
       ‚Ä¢ VERTICAL CANALS (crusArc/crusGrav/crusFling): after reaching the crus (œÜ‚â•phiExit‚àícrusArc) the otolith ENTERS
         THE CRUS CHAMBER and PARKS ‚Äî it leaves the sensitive canal, the cupula relaxes (Œæ‚Üí0, no nystagmus), it waits for
         expulsion into the utricle. Two physiological expulsion routes from the crus:
           ‚Äì GRAVITATIONAL (slow): with the head UPRIGHT (sitting) the utricle is below the crus ‚Üí the debris falls in. Condition
             ‚àígHead_y > crusGrav (projection of gravity onto the crus‚Üíutricle axis ‚âà [0,‚àí1,0]). Gives Epley expulsion AT
             SITTING (step 5) + a LIBERATORY nystagmus where it clinically occurs.
           ‚Äì INERTIAL (fast): an abrupt flip through a large angle (Semont ~180¬∞) throws the debris out of the crus by inertial
             forces regardless of gravity. Proxy: segment transition angle > crusFling (with a ~uniform transition
             time, a large angle ‚âà a large angular velocity). Sustains Semont expulsion AT THE FLING (step 3).
         Expulsion advances œÜ: (phiExit‚àícrusArc)‚ÜíphiExit over ~EXPEL_DUR(1.2 s) ‚Üí an ampullofugal nystagmus TRANSIENT
         (the SAME sign as the original) = liberatory/confirmatory. exited=true one-way (blocks the reversed phase).
       Backward motion in the canal lumen (dœÜ/dt<0, before the crus) ‚Üí Œæ<0 ‚Üí REVERSED nystagmus.
       VALIDATION (offline, real generated plans, both sides): Epley expulsion/liberatory at STEP 5 (sitting, Œæ~0.31);
       Semont at STEP 3 (fling); Yacovino at STEP 3 (chin to chest); horizontal canal (Lempert/Gufoni) UNCHANGED;
       Gufoni apo still does not clear (conversion). Diagnostics (a single lying position): the debris parks in the crus
       without expulsion ‚Üí nystagmus crescendo‚Üídecrescendo (extinguishes), the debris does NOT "vanish" (more physiological).
   CONSTANTS CALIBRATED TO THE LITERATURE (posterior canal, canalithiasis):
       latency ~1‚Äì3 s, time to peak ~7‚Äì9 s, attack duration ~25 s;
       tauC=5 s corresponds to a long cupular/canal time constant (~4‚Äì6 s).
   Returns: t [s], xi (sign = excitation/inhibition), phi [¬∞], exited (bool).
   Tuning parameters: tauP, tauC, gc, phiExit, fStat, adh.
   PARTICLE SIZE/DENSITY (size): "small" | "medium" (default) | "big" ‚Äî or a number (radius multiplier r).
       Mapping SIZE_R = {small:0.78, medium:1.0, big:1.35}. Physical scaling from r:
         tauP ‚àù r‚Åª¬≤  (Stokes settling velocity v ‚àù r¬≤ ‚Üí faster flow for a larger particle),
         gc   ‚àù r¬≥   (displaced volume/mass of endolymph ‚Üí cupular deflection),
         adh  ‚àù r    (detachment force of a sphere from the wall, JKR/DMT scaling).
       medium == r=1 ‚Üí result IDENTICAL to the previous calibration (safe default).
       Emergently: LARGE = shorter latency, stronger/faster nystagmus, faster exit;
       SMALL = long latency, weak and slow course, MAY NOT reach œÜ=178¬∞ in the shortened holds
       of a maneuver (e.g. Semont/Yacovino) ‚Äî consistent with the rationale of ~30 s holds for slowly settling
       fragments (Hain [2]) and with "extremely long-latency BPPV" [11]. Addresses the simplification
       "no effect of particle size" from the SIMPLIFICATIONS section.
   FATIGABILITY (rep) ‚Äî a DIFFERENTIATING criterion canalithiasis‚Üîcupulolithiasis [12].
       rep = the provocation repetition number in the same session (0 = first = full response). Mechanism:
       a compact cluster of otoconia DISPERSES on repetition (serial Dix‚ÄìHallpike) into smaller, less
       coherent fragments ‚Üí weaker aggregate cupular deflection. The factor fatigueFactor(rep,{fatTau,fatFloor})
       scales gc ‚Üí the AMPLITUDE Œæ (and the nystagmus) decreases linearly; GEOMETRY/LATENCY UNCHANGED (the dominant,
       undisputed clinical effect is the amplitude drop). Defaults fatTau=2.0, fatFloor=0.06; rep=0 ‚Üí factor 1
       ‚Üí IDENTICAL result (backward compatibility). Example (posterior canal): peak Œæ 0.81‚Üí0.51‚Üí0.33‚Üí0.22‚Üí0.15 for
       rep 0..4. CUPULOLITHIASIS (simulateCupulolith) has NO rep ‚Äî the debris adheres to the cupula, does not disperse
       ‚Üí does NOT fatigue; the difference is an emergent differentiating feature.

fatigueFactor(rep=0, {fatTau=2.0, fatFloor=0.06}) -> factor ‚àà [fatFloor, 1]
   Pure fatigability factor: fatFloor + (1‚àífatFloor)¬∑exp(‚àírep/fatTau); rep 0/negative/NaN ‚Üí 1; monotonic
   decay to fatFloor; fatTau‚â§0 ‚Üí RangeError. Exported to UI/diagnostics (serial Dix‚ÄìHallpike).

nystagmusPhase(xi, thr=0.05) -> "primary" | "reversed" | "none"
   Classification of the phase relative to the provoking direction:
   "primary" (Œæ>thr, primary/liberatory), "reversed" (Œæ<-thr, reversed), "none".

simulateCupulolith({canal, side, timeline, dt=0.05, tauCup=0.8, gain=1.0, size="medium"}) -> [{t, xi, target}, ...]
   size scales gain ‚àù r¬≥ (a heavier otoconia cluster ‚Üí stronger cupular deflection); no latency
   (tauCup unchanged). SIZE_R as in simulateCanalith. medium == r=1 ‚Üí identical result.
   Simulation of CUPULOLITHIASIS: otoliths attached to the cupula ‚Üí a heavy cupula deflected
   DIRECTLY by gravity (static projection), without a free particle in the canal lumen.
   Model:
       target = CUP_WEAK ¬∑ gain ¬∑ |proj| ¬∑ (excited ? +1 : -1)
                where proj/excited from position({canal,side,variant:"cupulo",q}) (Ewald's rule;
                horizontal: apogeotropic = reversed relative to geotropic)
                CUP_WEAK=0.6 ‚Äî CUPULOLITHIASIS WEAKER THAN CANALITHIASIS [1] (debris attached to the cupula
                deflects it more weakly than a bolus of free otoconia driving the endolymph column). The SAME
                coefficient scales the diagnostic nystagmus of the "cupulo" variant (nysFromGeom) ‚Äî a single source of truth.
       dŒæ/dt = (target - Œæ) / tauCup           (fast relaxation)
   Emergent features (contrast to canalithiasis):
       - NO LATENCY (Œæ follows gravity immediately; tauCup small)
       - PERSISTENCE (Œæ holds the target as long as the position is maintained; no extinction)
       - SMALLER AMPLITUDE (CUP_WEAK): nystagmus less intense than in canalithiasis [1]
       - smooth reversal on position change
   Returns: t [s], xi (sign = excitation/inhibition), target (static target).
   Tuning parameters: tauCup, gain ‚Äî TO BE CALIBRATED.

dynNystagmus(canal, side, xi) -> {excited, intensity, h, v, t}
   Conversion of instantaneous Œæ (from simulateCanalith) into nystagmus components.
   excited = (xi>0); direction = quickPhase with the sign of excitation.
   intensity = min(1, |xi| ¬∑ (excited ? 1 : 0.45))
               ‚Äî Ewald II rectification: the inhibitory response is weaker.
   [2026-07-02] NOW THE SOLE SOURCE of nystagmus direction/strength/reversal in the ENTIRE application ‚Äî diagnostics
   (via nysFromGeom‚Üíposition) AND maneuvers (via the view: nysFromDyn/stepXiPeak in otorepo.html, outside
   the Vestibular module). Manual direction annotations for maneuvers (headNys.ear/intensity) REMOVED;
   direction+strength+reversal (Œæ<0‚Üíinhibition) follow exclusively from this function. Validated by an Ewald
   audit for all maneuvers/sides (view_doc.txt: "ENGINE AUDIT AGAINST EWALD'S LAWS").

 INTERNAL CONSTANTS AND HELPERS (non-exported)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
qmul, qconj, qaxis(ax,deg), rotv(q,v), dot3, nrm3, nrm4 ‚Äî vector/quaternion algebra.
slerpQ(a,b,t) ‚Äî spherical quaternion interpolation (with hemisphere handling and the near case).
Q_SUPINE = qaxis([1,0,0],-100) ‚Äî base of the supine head-hanging orientation.

GEXC ‚Äî excitation axes of the VERTICAL canals (in the canal plane; g¬∑GEXC>0 = ampullofugal; axis = -e1):
   RP: norm(1,0,-1)  LP: norm(-1,0,-1)  RA: norm(-1,0,-1)  LA: norm(1,0,-1)   (anterior = -e1, consistent with CANAL_GEOM / the RESOLVED fix below)
uHC(side) ‚Äî the ampullopetal axis of the HORIZONTAL canal (interaural + antero-posterior):
   P: norm(0.87,0,0.5)   L: norm(-0.87,0,0.5)
CK ‚Äî mapping {canal,side} ‚Üí GEXC key for the vertical canals.

CANAL_GEOM ‚Äî arc geometry for the dynamics (e1=ampulla direction œÜ=0, e2=second plane axis,
             exc=sign of excitation):
   posterior  P:{e1 norm(-1,0,1),  e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}   L:{e1 norm(1,0,1),  e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}
   horizontal P:{e1 norm(0.87,0,0.5), e2 norm(0.5,0,-0.87), exc -1}
              L:{e1 norm(-0.87,0,0.5), e2 norm(-0.5,0,-0.87), exc -1}
   anterior   P:{e1 norm(1,0,1),   e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}   L:{e1 norm(-1,0,1), e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}
   Note: the ampullae of the posterior and anterior canals are on opposite sides (e1 reversed) ‚Äî
   a result of calibration to ampullofugal flow in the provoking position.

// Statically (diagnostics): nystagmus direction in a right-side Dix-Hallpike

                     q: Vestibular.qSupineYaw(45)});

   {q:[1,0,0,0], tTrans:0, tHold:2},
   {q:Vestibular.qSupineYaw(45), tTrans:1.5, tHold:25}
]});
Vestibular.dynNystagmus("posterior","P", tr[tr.length-1].xi);

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 BIBLIOGRAPHY AND MODEL CONSISTENCY WITH THE LITERATURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
The engine is a REDUCED, phenomenological implementation of established biomechanical
BPPV models ‚Äî it contains no new scientific hypotheses. Mapping:

[1] Squires TM, Weidman MS, Hain TC, Stone HA (2004). "A mathematical model
    for top-shelf vertigo: the role of sedimenting otoconia in BPPV."
    J Biomech 37(8):1137-1146.
    ‚Üí The canonical canalithiasis model: an otolith settling under gravity with Stokes
      drag, transcupular pressure, cupular deflection. CONSISTENT with our
      dynamics layer (simulateCanalith), in a simplified version.
    ‚Üí The MAIN mechanism of latency per [1]: no significant pressure until the otolith
      travels from the wide ampulla to the NARROW duct (pressure amplification).
      WE DO NOT MODEL THIS ‚Äî see the latency annotation below.

[2] Hain TC, Squires TM, Stone HA (2005). "Clinical implications of a
    mathematical model of BPPV." Ann N Y Acad Sci 1039:384-394.
    ‚Üí Clinical implications of [1]; the rationale for holding a position ~30 s in the CRP.

[3] House MG, Honrubia V (2003). "Theoretical models for the mechanisms of BPPV."
    ‚Üí Both mechanisms (canalithiasis and cupulolithiasis) are physically plausible.

[4] Obrist D, Hegemann S (2008). "Fluid-particle dynamics in canalithiasis."
    J R Soc Interface 5(27):1215-1229.
    ‚Üí Fluid‚Äìparticle dynamics; the open question of whether otoliths adhere to the wall.

[5] Dai M, Klein A, Cohen B, Raphan T (1999). "Model-based study of the human

ANNOTATION ‚Äî LATENCY (a deliberate modeling choice):
  Our latency arises from otolith ADHESION (a bond broken by a sustained tangential
  force). In the literature this is an ACCEPTED but MINORITY hypothesis, invoked
  especially for long latencies (Hain/dizziness-and-balance; "Extremely Long
  Latency BPPV" 2017). The MAINSTREAM [1,2] explains latency by the ampulla‚Üínarrow-duct
  geometry (pressure amplification), which we do NOT implement. Other hypotheses:
  inertia of the otolith clot. We reproduce the correct clinical behavior
  (latency present, shorter with a stronger provocation) by an alternative route.

GOAL AND PHILOSOPHY ("first-principles")
 We do not code the APPEARANCE of a pathology, but the PHYSIOLOGY: the resting activity of the labyrinths,
 Ewald's law with an inhibitory cut-off, fixation suppression (flocculus), canal VOR gain,
 the neural integrator, and the graviceptive axis (otoliths/MLF). The signs ‚Äî spontaneous
 nystagmus, its dependence on fixation and gaze, the head impulse (vHIT) result,
 skew deviation ‚Äî FOLLOW from the asymmetry of a few parameters. A layer INDEPENDENT of
 the BPPV dynamics (simulateCanalith); it shares only the direction conventions (quickPhase)
 and the frontal camera (mirror cam.right[0] = -1, like nysFromGeom/nysFromDyn).
 It realizes the todo item: "Other states (excited:false / push-pull): unilateral
 vestibulopathy, central nystagmus".

 Slow-phase velocities (SPV) and drift in ¬∞/s. Gaze angle gazeDeg: + = to the right (P).
 The "head-frame" sign h/t as in Vestibular; `dir`/`tdir` = the SCREEN sign after camera mirroring.

SIMPLIFICATIONS / CAVEATS:
  - HORIZONTAL UVH nystagmus: torsion as a fixed fraction of the horizontal (TORS_FRAC=0.5) ‚Äî illustrative; the VERTICAL canals
    (stage 7/E3) are composed realistically PER coplanar PAIR (verticalBeat), but with a simplified TORS_V=0.8;
    the HINTS RENDERER draws horizontal + VERTICAL + torsion (added in U4: neuroNysParams Av/vDir; the inferior-nerve downbeat visible);
    the animation is GATED by the visibility threshold (strength < VIS_THRESH/SPV_MAX ‚Üí 0), so as not to show sub-threshold
    gaze-evoked drift of the healthy (œÑ=25 ‚Üí 0.8¬∞/s) ‚Äî consistent with the label (spv<VIS_THRESH = "no nystagmus");
  - vHIT: a single gain value (the excited canal); covert/overt saccades as a TEMPORAL SPLIT of the total
    correction (share from the neck-ocular fusion), without the full eyeball velocity dynamics;
  - a linear integrator (drift Œ∏/œÑ) without horizontal/vertical asymmetry and without rebound nystagmus;
  - skew as a scalar vertical divergence; no full OTR geometry (head tilt, cyclotorsion);
  - compensation: a single scalar c (without a time axis hours‚Üîweeks); clamp/pacemaker as pure functions of c;
    the pacemaker charge (Bechterew) as a sticky parameter (a carrier of history), because the scalar c carries no memory;
  - neck-ocular fusion and velocity storage: phenomenological (weight ‚àù reliability / c), without a full Kalman
    filter; head-shaking nystagmus omitted (a latent velocity-storage asymmetry after compensation);
  - HINTS as an EDUCATIONAL model ‚Äî it does NOT replace an examination; valid in AVS with a persistent nystagmus,
    interpret with HINTS "plus" (hearing) and context (Tarnutzer 2011 [H9]). NOTE: a compensated peripheral loss
    can have a bedside HIT "normal" (covert saccades) ‚Äî a pitfall when the AVS nystagmus is no longer present.
