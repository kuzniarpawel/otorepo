This document describes the public and internal helper API of the engine and the adopted
conventions. Keep it updated as the engine evolves.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 CONVENTIONS AND COORDINATE SYSTEMS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Head frame:  x = right, y = up (skull), z = forward (nose).
World: "up" axis = +y; world gravity = (0,-1,0).
Sides (ear/side): "L" = left ear, "P" = right ear.
Canals (canal): "posterior", "horizontal", "anterior".

Quaternion: q = [w, x, y, z]; rotates HEAD vectors → WORLD
            (v_world = q ⊗ v_head ⊗ q*).

Nystagmus components (anatomical, patient frame), returned as {h, v, t}:
   h: + to the right / − to the left        (horizontal)
   v: + upward / − downward         (vertical)
   t: + upper poles to the right / − to the left  (torsional)

Ewald's laws used in the engine:
   I   — plane specificity: the fast-phase axis of a given canal (quickPhase)
   II  — horizontal canal: AMPULLOPETAL flow is excitatory; excitation > inhibition
   III — vertical canals: AMPULLOFUGAL flow is excitatory

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 PUBLIC API (returned from the module)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

— STAGE 0 (rule-based) ——————————————————————————————————————————————

isExcitatory(canal, flow) -> bool
   Whether the given flow direction excites the canal (Ewald's law II/III).
   flow: "ampullopetal" | "ampullofugal".
   Returns: horizontal → (flow==="ampullopetal"); vertical → (flow==="ampullofugal").

quickPhase(canal, ear) -> {h, v, t}
   Fast-phase axis for canal EXCITATION, ipsiversive to the ear (Ewald I).
   ear: "L"|"P". (ipsi = ear==="P" ? +1 : -1)
   horizontal → {h:ipsi, v:0,    t:0}      (horizontal toward the excited ear; torsion masked)
   posterior  → {h:0,    v:+0.93,t:ipsi}   (upbeat + torsion toward the ear; real v:t≈0.93:1)
   anterior   → {h:0,    v:-1,   t:0}      (PURE downbeat; geometric torsion ~0.78 MASKED — NYS_SHOW.anterior.t=false)
   NOTE — CLINICAL MASK (NYS_SHOW): quickPhase returns the UI convention for BPPV (anterior = pure downbeat,
   horizontal without torsion). The FULL geometry (anterior torsion PRESERVED, for lateralization) → NeuroVOR.qpFull()
   / Vestibular.nysMag(); used by the HINTS layer (vHIT saccade / verticalBeat / SCDS). See the "CLINICAL MASK" section.

nysMag(canal, ear) -> {h, v, t}
   Full geometric proportions of the nystagmus from REAL canal normals (CANAL_NORMALS), normalized
   to max=1. Does NOT apply the NYS_SHOW clinical mask (anterior-canal torsion PRESERVED) — cf. quickPhase
   (same geometry, masked to the BPPV convention). Source of the full geometry for NeuroVOR
   (qpFull → vHIT saccade / verticalBeat / pressureStimulus-SCDS). ear: "L"|"P".

nystagmus({canal, ear, excited=true}) -> {h, v, t}
   Fast phase for the given, excited/inhibited canal.
   Inhibition (excited=false) reverses the sign of all components.
   Future use: unilateral loss (excited:false), push-pull.

— STAGE 1 (static geometry) ————————————————————————————————————

gHead(q) -> [x, y, z]
   The gravity vector expressed in the head frame for orientation q.
   Upright-standing head (q=[1,0,0,0]) → [0,-1,0].

qSupineYaw(deg) -> q
   "Supine head-hanging" orientation (a -100° pitch about the interaural axis)
   with a head yaw of `deg` about the skull axis (y). Use: Dix-Hallpike (±45),
   Roll (±90). Note: positive deg = right ear down (3D convention, NOT the UI schematic).

qPitch(deg) -> q
   Pure pitch about the interaural axis (x). +deg = bow forward (bow),
   -deg = lean back (lean). Use: Bow & Lean test.

position({canal, side, variant, q}) -> {excited, mag, h, v, t}
   Static stimulation of the AFFECTED canal in head orientation q.
   side: "L"|"P"; variant: "canalo" (geotropic) | "cupulo" (apogeotropic).
   Mechanism: projection of gravity onto the canal's excitation axis → flow → Ewald.
     - horizontal:  proj = g · uHC(side);  excited = (variant==="cupulo" ? proj<0 : proj>0)
                 (Ewald II: geo/apo reverse)
     - vertical:  proj = g · GEXC[canal];  excited = proj>0
                 (Ewald III; no geo/apo reversal for vertical canals)
   excited: bool; mag = |proj| (raw force); {h,v,t} = quickPhase with the sign of excitation.
   VALIDATED: Dix-Hallpike (posterior), Roll (geo/apo), Bow & Lean.

— STAGE 2 (particle dynamics) ————————————————————————————————————————

simulateCanalith({canal, side, timeline, q0=null, dt=0.05, tauP=6.5, tauC=5, gc=1.6, phiExit=178, fStat=0.04, adh=0.2, size="medium", rep=0, fatTau=2.0, fatFloor=0.06, crusArc=12, crusGrav=0.6, crusFling=145}) -> [{t, xi, phi, exited}, ...]
   Simulation of CANALITHIASIS: the otolith as a particle on the canal arc.
   [PHENOMENOLOGICAL MODEL — a proxy for the dynamics, not full hydrodynamics; see the "MODEL NATURE" header
    and "SIMPLIFICATIONS RELATIVE TO FULL MODELS".]
   timeline: array of segments [{q, tTrans, tHold}, ...]
       q      — target orientation (quaternion)
       tTrans — transition time to q [s] (slerp interpolation from the previous q)
       tHold  — hold time at q [s]
   q0 — STARTING orientation (quaternion); default null = the q of the FIRST segment (backward compatibility).
       NOTE (semantics of the 1st segment): with q0=null the first segment does NOT interpolate from the neutral position —
       its tTrans is time ALREADY IN the starting position, NOT a transition from sitting. To force a transition from a given
       orientation to the 1st q, pass q0 (e.g. [1,0,0,0] = neutral head). UI convention: the 1st segment has tTrans:0,
       and the transition is realized by the NEXT segment (maneuverTimeline: tTrans:0.8 per step). Analogously for simulateCupulolith.
   Model:
       φ — arc angle of the particle (ampulla φ=0, exit/common crus φ≈180°); lower bound 3°.
       Overdamped motion (Stokes drag, no inertia):
           dφ/dt = (g_head · tangent(φ)) / tauP
       Cupula (first-order model, long time constant):
           dξ/dt = -ξ/tauC + gc · exc · dφ/dt   (exc: +1 vertical / -1 horizontal)
   OTOLITH ADHESION (latency):
       The otolith is initially attached; the bond (adh) is broken by a sustained tangential force
       above the threshold fStat → a delayed start = LATENCY. A stronger provocation = shorter
       latency (consistent with the clinic). Once broken, the otolith no longer re-attaches in this session.
   COMMON-CRUS CHAMBER + EXPULSION INTO THE UTRICLE (VERTICAL canals: posterior+anterior; the horizontal has no crus):
       Anatomically, "reaching the end of the arc" (φ=phiExit) ≠ "falling into the utricle" — these are TWO events.
       • HORIZONTAL CANAL: no common crus → the non-ampullary end opens DIRECTLY into the utricle: after φ≥phiExit
         the otolith enters the utricle ONE-WAY (exited). (Unchanged from the previous version.)
       • VERTICAL CANALS (crusArc/crusGrav/crusFling): after reaching the crus (φ≥phiExit−crusArc) the otolith ENTERS
         THE CRUS CHAMBER and PARKS — it leaves the sensitive canal, the cupula relaxes (ξ→0, no nystagmus), it waits for
         expulsion into the utricle. Two physiological expulsion routes from the crus:
           – GRAVITATIONAL (slow): with the head UPRIGHT (sitting) the utricle is below the crus → the debris falls in. Condition
             −gHead_y > crusGrav (projection of gravity onto the crus→utricle axis ≈ [0,−1,0]). Gives Epley expulsion AT
             SITTING (step 5) + a LIBERATORY nystagmus where it clinically occurs.
           – INERTIAL (fast): an abrupt flip through a large angle (Semont ~180°) throws the debris out of the crus by inertial
             forces regardless of gravity. Proxy: segment transition angle > crusFling (with a ~uniform transition
             time, a large angle ≈ a large angular velocity). Sustains Semont expulsion AT THE FLING (step 3).
         Expulsion advances φ: (phiExit−crusArc)→phiExit over ~EXPEL_DUR(1.2 s) → an ampullofugal nystagmus TRANSIENT
         (the SAME sign as the original) = liberatory/confirmatory. exited=true one-way (blocks the reversed phase).
       Backward motion in the canal lumen (dφ/dt<0, before the crus) → ξ<0 → REVERSED nystagmus.
       VALIDATION (offline, real generated plans, both sides): Epley expulsion/liberatory at STEP 5 (sitting, ξ~0.31);
       Semont at STEP 3 (fling); Yacovino at STEP 3 (chin to chest); horizontal canal (Lempert/Gufoni) UNCHANGED;
       Gufoni apo still does not clear (conversion). Diagnostics (a single lying position): the debris parks in the crus
       without expulsion → nystagmus crescendo→decrescendo (extinguishes), the debris does NOT "vanish" (more physiological).
   CONSTANTS CALIBRATED TO THE LITERATURE (posterior canal, canalithiasis):
       latency ~1–3 s, time to peak ~7–9 s, attack duration ~25 s;
       tauC=5 s corresponds to a long cupular/canal time constant (~4–6 s).
   Returns: t [s], xi (sign = excitation/inhibition), phi [°], exited (bool).
   Tuning parameters: tauP, tauC, gc, phiExit, fStat, adh.
   PARTICLE SIZE/DENSITY (size): "small" | "medium" (default) | "big" — or a number (radius multiplier r).
       Mapping SIZE_R = {small:0.78, medium:1.0, big:1.35}. Physical scaling from r:
         tauP ∝ r⁻²  (Stokes settling velocity v ∝ r² → faster flow for a larger particle),
         gc   ∝ r³   (displaced volume/mass of endolymph → cupular deflection),
         adh  ∝ r    (detachment force of a sphere from the wall, JKR/DMT scaling).
       medium == r=1 → result IDENTICAL to the previous calibration (safe default).
       Emergently: LARGE = shorter latency, stronger/faster nystagmus, faster exit;
       SMALL = long latency, weak and slow course, MAY NOT reach φ=178° in the shortened holds
       of a maneuver (e.g. Semont/Yacovino) — consistent with the rationale of ~30 s holds for slowly settling
       fragments (Hain [2]) and with "extremely long-latency BPPV" [11]. Addresses the simplification
       "no effect of particle size" from the SIMPLIFICATIONS section.
   FATIGABILITY (rep) — a DIFFERENTIATING criterion canalithiasis↔cupulolithiasis [12].
       rep = the provocation repetition number in the same session (0 = first = full response). Mechanism:
       a compact cluster of otoconia DISPERSES on repetition (serial Dix–Hallpike) into smaller, less
       coherent fragments → weaker aggregate cupular deflection. The factor fatigueFactor(rep,{fatTau,fatFloor})
       scales gc → the AMPLITUDE ξ (and the nystagmus) decreases linearly; GEOMETRY/LATENCY UNCHANGED (the dominant,
       undisputed clinical effect is the amplitude drop). Defaults fatTau=2.0, fatFloor=0.06; rep=0 → factor 1
       → IDENTICAL result (backward compatibility). Example (posterior canal): peak ξ 0.81→0.51→0.33→0.22→0.15 for
       rep 0..4. CUPULOLITHIASIS (simulateCupulolith) has NO rep — the debris adheres to the cupula, does not disperse
       → does NOT fatigue; the difference is an emergent differentiating feature.

fatigueFactor(rep=0, {fatTau=2.0, fatFloor=0.06}) -> factor ∈ [fatFloor, 1]
   Pure fatigability factor: fatFloor + (1−fatFloor)·exp(−rep/fatTau); rep 0/negative/NaN → 1; monotonic
   decay to fatFloor; fatTau≤0 → RangeError. Exported to UI/diagnostics (serial Dix–Hallpike).

nystagmusPhase(xi, thr=0.05) -> "primary" | "reversed" | "none"
   Classification of the phase relative to the provoking direction:
   "primary" (ξ>thr, primary/liberatory), "reversed" (ξ<-thr, reversed), "none".

simulateCupulolith({canal, side, timeline, dt=0.05, tauCup=0.8, gain=1.0, size="medium"}) -> [{t, xi, target}, ...]
   size scales gain ∝ r³ (a heavier otoconia cluster → stronger cupular deflection); no latency
   (tauCup unchanged). SIZE_R as in simulateCanalith. medium == r=1 → identical result.
   Simulation of CUPULOLITHIASIS: otoliths attached to the cupula → a heavy cupula deflected
   DIRECTLY by gravity (static projection), without a free particle in the canal lumen.
   Model:
       target = CUP_WEAK · gain · |proj| · (excited ? +1 : -1)
                where proj/excited from position({canal,side,variant:"cupulo",q}) (Ewald's rule;
                horizontal: apogeotropic = reversed relative to geotropic)
                CUP_WEAK=0.6 — CUPULOLITHIASIS WEAKER THAN CANALITHIASIS [1] (debris attached to the cupula
                deflects it more weakly than a bolus of free otoconia driving the endolymph column). The SAME
                coefficient scales the diagnostic nystagmus of the "cupulo" variant (nysFromGeom) — a single source of truth.
       dξ/dt = (target - ξ) / tauCup           (fast relaxation)
   Emergent features (contrast to canalithiasis):
       - NO LATENCY (ξ follows gravity immediately; tauCup small)
       - PERSISTENCE (ξ holds the target as long as the position is maintained; no extinction)
       - SMALLER AMPLITUDE (CUP_WEAK): nystagmus less intense than in canalithiasis [1]
       - smooth reversal on position change
   Returns: t [s], xi (sign = excitation/inhibition), target (static target).
   Tuning parameters: tauCup, gain — TO BE CALIBRATED.

dynNystagmus(canal, side, xi) -> {excited, intensity, h, v, t}
   Conversion of instantaneous ξ (from simulateCanalith) into nystagmus components.
   excited = (xi>0); direction = quickPhase with the sign of excitation.
   intensity = min(1, |xi| · (excited ? 1 : 0.45))
               — Ewald II rectification: the inhibitory response is weaker.
   [2026-07-02] NOW THE SOLE SOURCE of nystagmus direction/strength/reversal in the ENTIRE application — diagnostics
   (via nysFromGeom→position) AND maneuvers (via the view: nysFromDyn/stepXiPeak in otorepo.html, outside
   the Vestibular module). Manual direction annotations for maneuvers (headNys.ear/intensity) REMOVED;
   direction+strength+reversal (ξ<0→inhibition) follow exclusively from this function. Validated by an Ewald
   audit for all maneuvers/sides (view_doc.txt: "ENGINE AUDIT AGAINST EWALD'S LAWS").

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 INTERNAL CONSTANTS AND HELPERS (non-exported)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
qmul, qconj, qaxis(ax,deg), rotv(q,v), dot3, nrm3, nrm4 — vector/quaternion algebra.
slerpQ(a,b,t) — spherical quaternion interpolation (with hemisphere handling and the near case).
Q_SUPINE = qaxis([1,0,0],-100) — base of the supine head-hanging orientation.

GEXC — excitation axes of the VERTICAL canals (in the canal plane; g·GEXC>0 = ampullofugal; axis = -e1):
   RP: norm(1,0,-1)  LP: norm(-1,0,-1)  RA: norm(-1,0,-1)  LA: norm(1,0,-1)   (anterior = -e1, consistent with CANAL_GEOM / the RESOLVED fix below)
uHC(side) — the ampullopetal axis of the HORIZONTAL canal (interaural + antero-posterior):
   P: norm(0.87,0,0.5)   L: norm(-0.87,0,0.5)
CK — mapping {canal,side} → GEXC key for the vertical canals.

CANAL_GEOM — arc geometry for the dynamics (e1=ampulla direction φ=0, e2=second plane axis,
             exc=sign of excitation):
   posterior  P:{e1 norm(-1,0,1),  e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}   L:{e1 norm(1,0,1),  e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}
   horizontal P:{e1 norm(0.87,0,0.5), e2 norm(0.5,0,-0.87), exc -1}
              L:{e1 norm(-0.87,0,0.5), e2 norm(-0.5,0,-0.87), exc -1}
   anterior   P:{e1 norm(1,0,1),   e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}   L:{e1 norm(-1,0,1), e2 (0,-1,0), exc +1}
   Note: the ampullae of the posterior and anterior canals are on opposite sides (e1 reversed) —
   a result of calibration to ampullofugal flow in the provoking position.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
 BIBLIOGRAPHY AND MODEL CONSISTENCY WITH THE LITERATURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
The engine is a REDUCED, phenomenological implementation of established biomechanical
BPPV models — it contains no new scientific hypotheses. Mapping:

[1] Squires TM, Weidman MS, Hain TC, Stone HA (2004). "A mathematical model
    for top-shelf vertigo: the role of sedimenting otoconia in BPPV."
    J Biomech 37(8):1137-1146.
    → The canonical canalithiasis model: an otolith settling under gravity with Stokes
      drag, transcupular pressure, cupular deflection. CONSISTENT with our
      dynamics layer (simulateCanalith), in a simplified version.
    → The MAIN mechanism of latency per [1]: no significant pressure until the otolith
      travels from the wide ampulla to the NARROW duct (pressure amplification).
      WE DO NOT MODEL THIS — see the latency annotation below.

[2] Hain TC, Squires TM, Stone HA (2005). "Clinical implications of a
    mathematical model of BPPV." Ann N Y Acad Sci 1039:384-394.
    → Clinical implications of [1]; the rationale for holding a position ~30 s in the CRP.

[3] House MG, Honrubia V (2003). "Theoretical models for the mechanisms of BPPV."
    → Both mechanisms (canalithiasis and cupulolithiasis) are physically plausible.

[4] Obrist D, Hegemann S (2008). "Fluid-particle dynamics in canalithiasis."
    J R Soc Interface 5(27):1215-1229.
    → Fluid–particle dynamics; the open question of whether otoliths adhere to the wall.

[5] Dai M, Klein A, Cohen B, Raphan T (1999). "Model-based study of the human
    cupular time constant." J Vestib Res.
    → Long cupular/canal time constant (~4–6 s). CONSISTENT with tauC=5.

[6] Schuknecht HF (1969). Cupulolithiasis (heavy cupula → immediate,
    persistent nystagmus). CONSISTENT with simulateCupulolith (no latency, persistence).

[7] Hall SF, Ruby RRF, McClure JA (1979). Canalithiasis (free fragments in the canal).
    CONSISTENT with the particle model. (cf. Brandt & Steddin 1993 — canalithiasis better
    explains latency and fatigability).

[8] Steinhausen (1933); Van Egmond, Groen, Jongkees (1949) — the torsional-pendulum
    model of the cupula. CONSISTENT with the first-order cupula.

[9] Ewald (1892) — Ewald's laws I/II/III. CONSISTENT with quickPhase / isExcitatory.

[10] Della Santina et al. (2005) — canal-plane orientations. The basis for the
     canal normals (GEXC, CANAL_GEOM) in an idealized version (45°).

[11] "Extremely Long Latency BPPV" (Res Vestib Sci, 2017) — very long latencies
     attributed to otolith ADHESION and their size. The basis for the adhesion hypothesis
     (our latency) as a minority mechanism.

[12] Dix MR, Hallpike CS (1952) — the original description of positional BPPV nystagmus and its
     FATIGABILITY (extinction/attenuation on repeated provocation). The dispersion mechanism
     of the compact debris: Brandt & Steddin (1993, [7]). The basis for the fatigueFactor (rep) factor:
     canalithiasis fatigues, cupulolithiasis (debris on the cupula) — does not.

ANNOTATION — LATENCY (a deliberate modeling choice):
  Our latency arises from otolith ADHESION (a bond broken by a sustained tangential
  force). In the literature this is an ACCEPTED but MINORITY hypothesis, invoked
  especially for long latencies (Hain/dizziness-and-balance; "Extremely Long
  Latency BPPV" 2017). The MAINSTREAM [1,2] explains latency by the ampulla→narrow-duct
  geometry (pressure amplification), which we do NOT implement. Other hypotheses:
  inertia of the otolith clot. We reproduce the correct clinical behavior
  (latency present, shorter with a stronger provocation) by an alternative route.

SIMPLIFICATIONS RELATIVE TO FULL MODELS:
  - a "tangential gravity → flow" proxy instead of the full transcupular pressure ΔPc;
  - no amplification in the narrow duct (the main latency mechanism per [1]);
  - particle size: SUPPORTED as a scaling parameter (size → tauP∝r⁻², gc∝r³, adh∝r);
    still no wall interactions and no velocity storage;
  - cupulolithiasis WEAKER than canalithiasis [1]: MODELED by a single coefficient CUP_WEAK=0.6
    (amplitude multiplier of ξ in simulateCupulolith AND of the diagnostic nystagmus of the "cupulo" variant in nysFromGeom);
    residual simplification: the coefficient is GLOBAL (not per-canal or per-geometry), a calibration value;
  - fatigability (dispersion of the compact debris on repetition) MODELED as a factor scaling
    amplitude (fatigueFactor / the rep parameter in simulateCanalith) — NOT as an explicit simulation of many
    particles with different trajectories; cupulolithiasis is deliberately without fatigability (a differentiating feature) [12].


=== MANEUVERS AS QUATERNION TIMELINES (entry into the dynamics) ===
The maneuver plan → engine bridge. simulateCanalith uses ONLY gHead (gravity in the head frame),
so we define a step by the target gHead and build a quaternion qFromG(g).
Head frame: x=right ear, y=skull(+crown), z=nose.
Functions (after xiEnvelope in the file):
- qFromG(g) → a head→world quaternion such that gHead(q)=g.
- rotYg(g,deg) → rotation of gravity about the skull axis (neck yaw).
- BASE_G[body|face] → gHead for yaw=0 (sit/sideL/sideR/prone/sitFront). Yaw: rotYg(g,-yaw).
- LEAN_G[leanL/R|up/down] → FINAL gHead for the Semont flings (neck yaw built in; mirrored by side).

GOAL AND PHILOSOPHY ("first-principles")
 We do not code the APPEARANCE of a pathology, but the PHYSIOLOGY: the resting activity of the labyrinths,
 Ewald's law with an inhibitory cut-off, fixation suppression (flocculus), canal VOR gain,
 the neural integrator, and the graviceptive axis (otoliths/MLF). The signs — spontaneous
 nystagmus, its dependence on fixation and gaze, the head impulse (vHIT) result,
 skew deviation — FOLLOW from the asymmetry of a few parameters. A layer INDEPENDENT of
 the BPPV dynamics (simulateCanalith); it shares only the direction conventions (quickPhase)
 and the frontal camera (mirror cam.right[0] = -1, like nysFromGeom/nysFromDyn).
 It realizes the todo item: "Other states (excited:false / push-pull): unilateral
 vestibulopathy, central nystagmus".

CONVENTIONS
 Sides: "P"=right=+x (right ear), "L"=left=-x. Afferent frequencies in Hz.
 Slow-phase velocities (SPV) and drift in °/s. Gaze angle gazeDeg: + = to the right (P).
 The "head-frame" sign h/t as in Vestibular; `dir`/`tdir` = the SCREEN sign after camera mirroring.

PUBLIC API (returned from the module)

 afferent(tone, drive) -> Hz                                                                  [H1][H2]
   The afferent after RECTIFICATION: max(0, min(R_SAT, tone+drive)). Inhibitory cut-off (≥0) +
   saturation (≤R_SAT=300). A shared primitive: rest (drive=0) and head impulse (drive≠0).

 spontaneous(p) -> {kind,h,v,t,dir,tdir,vdir,strength,spv,beatEar,lesionEar}                  [H1][H2]
   STAGE 1 — SPONTANEOUS nystagmus from tonic asymmetry (push-pull). The brain estimates head velocity
   from the difference of the horizontal-pair afferents: Ω_est ∝ (rateL − rateR). A tonic asymmetry mimics a steady
   rotation → the SLOW phase toward the weaker labyrinth, the FAST phase (the visible beat) toward the stronger one.
   spv = SPV_MAX·|toneL−toneR|/R0 (SPV_MAX=12°/s, R0=90). Horizontal-torsional nystagmus (tH = 0.5·h).
   Direction FIXED regardless of gaze — a PERIPHERAL feature.
   STAGE 7 (E3): a VERTICAL-TORSIONAL component from the vertical-canal pairs (verticalBeat) is added to the horizontal —
   fields v/vdir, tV, t=tH+tV, spvV/strengthV; kind="verticalTorsional" when the vertical dominates. Vertical canals
   healthy → v=0, spvV=0 (the horizontal case unchanged). See verticalBeat (STAGE 7).

 suppressionFactor(p, fixOn) -> [0..~1.5]                                                       [H3]
 observe(p, fixOn) -> spontaneous(p) with suppression applied {spv,spvRaw,suppressionFactor,suppressed,...}
   STAGE 2 — FIXATION SUPPRESSION. eye = VOR − fixationGain·retinal_slip. fixOn=true (light):
   peripheral (fixationGain≈0.9) fades (×0.1); central (≈0) persists; <0 = paradoxical enhancement.
   fixOn=false (Frenzel/darkness): the full slow phase. `suppressed` = clinically "suppresses with fixation".

 headImpulse(p, toSide, {headVel=200, headAmp=15}) -> {gain,deficit,saccadePresent,saccadeAmp,
                                                        saccadeToSide,inhibitedFloored,abnormal,...}  [H4][H5]
   STAGE 3 — head impulse (vHIT). At the impulse velocity the INHIBITED canal hits the inhibitory cut-off
   (0 Hz, `inhibitedFloored`) → the VOR velocity is carried SOLELY by the EXCITED canal
   (ipsilateral). gain = the excited modulation / the ideal one. deficit → a corrective SACCADE (overt when
   ≥2.5° ≈ gain<0.83), directed opposite to the impulse (`saccadeToSide`). CENTRAL: gains≈1 →
   a normal HIT (ominous). Calibration S_HZ=0.8 → cut-off ~tone/S≈112°/s < the vHIT velocity.
   STAGE 7 (E2): the second argument is POLYMORPHIC — 'P'/'L' (horizontal, as above, numerically unchanged) OR {canal,ear}
   (any of the 6 canals; planes HC/RALP/LARP). It also returns canal/ear/plane/coplanar/saccade{h,v,t}. See vhitPlane.

 gazeEvoked(p, gazeDeg) -> {tau,spv,beatSign,driftVel,present}                                  [H6]

 directionChanging(p, fixOn, ecc=20) -> bool
   STAGE 4a — a "leaky" integrator: at gaze θ the drift toward center = θ/τ (slow phase), the fast phase
   toward the gaze. Healthy τ≈25 (negligible drift); central τ≈1–3 → a DIRECTION-CHANGING nystagmus.
   nystagmusAtGaze sums the beat velocities: SPONTANEOUS (peripheral, fixed) + GAZE-EVOKED (central, changing).
   directionChanging requires visibility ≥VIS_THRESH(2°/s) on BOTH sides and opposite directions
   (a strong spontaneous nystagmus can dominate the drift → it stays unidirectional — realistic).

 skew(p) -> {present,central,skewDeg,sign,hyperSide,torsionDeg}                                  [H7]
   STAGE 4b — skew deviation / OTR from graviceptive asymmetry (otoliths + MLF). present when |skewTone|≥1°.
   central when |skewTone|≥SKEW_CENTRAL(2.5°) — only a SIGNIFICANT skew counts as a CENTRAL feature (INFARCT);
   a small skew (e.g. the utricle of the superior nerve) present, but NOT central (avoids a false "stroke" — a review fix).
   Alternate-cover test: the uncovered eye catches fixation with a VERTICAL SACCADE = positive cover test = central.
 svv(p) -> {tiltSide, deg, abnormal}                                                             [H22]
   STAGE 7 — SUBJECTIVE VISUAL VERTICAL (SVV): the graviceptive tilt = f(utricle + VERTICAL canals AC/PC).
   The vertical tilts TOWARD THE AFFECTED SIDE (the graviceptively weaker one). MORE SENSITIVE than skew deviation: an
   INFERIOR neuritis gives ~2.5° of tilt DESPITE a normal skew (skew from the utricle=0). Symmetry (BVH)→0. abnormal when ≥2°.
   Does NOT affect the HINTS verdict (a supplementary readout).
 vemp(p) -> {cVEMP:{L,R,ampL,ampR,asym,weakEar,organ,nerve}, oVEMP:{...}}                         [H22]
   STAGE 7 — VEMP: cVEMP(cervical)≈SACCULE→INFERIOR nerve (ipsi); oVEMP(ocular)≈UTRICLE→SUPERIOR nerve. Amplitude from the
   organ's function (sacculeL/R, utricleL/R ∈0..1). Status: ≥0.65 normal / ≥0.3 reduced / <0.3 absent. It separates
   the branches: INFERIOR neuritis→cVEMP↓+oVEMP normal; SUPERIOR→the opposite; BVH→both bilaterally. weakEar = the weaker ear.

 SCENARIOS, scenario(key) -> patient                                                            [H8]
   Presets: normal · neuritisR/neuritisL (PERIPHERAL: tone↓ + gain↓ of the affected canal, flocculus intact,
   integrator normal, no skew) · strokeCentral (CENTRAL: canals INTACT gain≈1 → a normal HIT,
   mild asymmetric tone, a "leaky" integrator, skew+torsion). KEY: the STATIC tone (nystagmus) and the
   DYNAMIC gain (HIT) are SEPARATE parameters — that is why a stroke gives a nystagmus with a normal HIT.

 compensate(p) -> {vnL, vnR, c, lesionEar, clampAmt, paceAmt, pacemakerBias}                    [H10][H11]
   STAGE 6 — STATIC compensation. The activity of the vestibular NUCLEI (VN) after neuroplasticity — it is this
   (not the raw labyrinth) that push-pull "sees" (spontaneous). Two-track: a CEREBELLAR CLAMP lowers the healthy
   nucleus (early, contribution exp(−c/CLAMP_TAU)) + an intrinsic PACEMAKER raises the affected nucleus (late, persistent).
   clamp+pacemaker = c·gap (restored) → symmetry at c=1 WITHOUT overshoot. c=0 and bias=0 → an exact
   NO-OP (vnL/vnR = afferent(tone,0)) — consistent with stages 1–5.
   STAGE 6b — pacemakerBias (sticky, [Hz]): a latched pacemaker charge INDEPENDENT of the current tone.
   When the labyrinth recovers function, the affected nucleus (tone returned + charged bias) becomes STRONGER →
   a RECOVERY nystagmus (Bechterew's) beating toward the EARLIER affected ear. The scalar c carries no history —
   which is why the charge is a separate, persistent parameter (flag spontaneous().bechterew).

 fusionWeights(p, headVel, toSide) -> {wCanal, wNeck, canalRel, neckRel}                        [H12][H13][H14]
   STAGE 6c — RELIABILITY-WEIGHTED FUSION (precision-weighted / complementary filter; an optimal estimator).
   The canal carries a velocity estimate with a weight = reliability (gain × the margin to saturation). At high velocity
   the canal loses reliability (saturation/inhibitory cut-off) → the estimator SWITCHES to neck
   proprioception/efference: neckRel = c·(1−canalRel). The "switch" FOLLOWS from the weights, not from a script. fw describes
   the MECHANISM (when/why the system switches to prediction).

   (integratorTau, stage 4a): compensation shortens velocity storage, but NOT gaze-holding (otherwise a false central).

SIMPLIFICATIONS / CAVEATS:
  - HORIZONTAL UVH nystagmus: torsion as a fixed fraction of the horizontal (TORS_FRAC=0.5) — illustrative; the VERTICAL canals
    (stage 7/E3) are composed realistically PER coplanar PAIR (verticalBeat), but with a simplified TORS_V=0.8;
    the HINTS RENDERER draws horizontal + VERTICAL + torsion (added in U4: neuroNysParams Av/vDir; the inferior-nerve downbeat visible);
    the animation is GATED by the visibility threshold (strength < VIS_THRESH/SPV_MAX → 0), so as not to show sub-threshold
    gaze-evoked drift of the healthy (τ=25 → 0.8°/s) — consistent with the label (spv<VIS_THRESH = "no nystagmus");
  - vHIT: a single gain value (the excited canal); covert/overt saccades as a TEMPORAL SPLIT of the total
    correction (share from the neck-ocular fusion), without the full eyeball velocity dynamics;
  - a linear integrator (drift θ/τ) without horizontal/vertical asymmetry and without rebound nystagmus;
  - skew as a scalar vertical divergence; no full OTR geometry (head tilt, cyclotorsion);
  - compensation: a single scalar c (without a time axis hours↔weeks); clamp/pacemaker as pure functions of c;
    the pacemaker charge (Bechterew) as a sticky parameter (a carrier of history), because the scalar c carries no memory;
  - neck-ocular fusion and velocity storage: phenomenological (weight ∝ reliability / c), without a full Kalman
    filter; head-shaking nystagmus omitted (a latent velocity-storage asymmetry after compensation);
  - HINTS as an EDUCATIONAL model — it does NOT replace an examination; valid in AVS with a persistent nystagmus,
    interpret with HINTS "plus" (hearing) and context (Tarnutzer 2011 [H9]). NOTE: a compensated peripheral loss
    can have a bedside HIT "normal" (covert saccades) — a pitfall when the AVS nystagmus is no longer present.
