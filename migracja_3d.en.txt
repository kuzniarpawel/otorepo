COORDINATE FRAMES — BRIDGE (the most important math)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Our world:  x=right, y=up, z=FRONT (nose, belly). Gravity down=[0,-1,0].
Three.js:   x=right, y=up, z=BACK (toward the observer); an object's "front" is local -z.
NOTE: this is a mirror of the z axis — it is NOT enough to negate the z component of a vector, because it changes handedness.
PRINCIPLE: do NOT trust a ready-made formula. Build the head orientation in Three from BASIS VECTORS
  (where the nose and the vertex point in the world) and VERIFY with the same test we
  have been using: rotate the nose [0,0,1], the vertex [0,1,0], and gravity — check that they land where
  gHead(stepHeadQ) says. Only when the test passes for all 21 combinations
  (position×face) is the bridge ready. Candidate to check: q'=(-qx,-qy,qz,qw) (z mirror),
  but we CONFIRM it numerically, we do not assume it.

STAGES (each one leaves a working app)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 0 — Three offline in the file
  • Paweł supplies three.min.js to /mnt/user-data/uploads.
  • Merge step: three.min.js pasted into a <script> at the start of otorepo.html (inline).
  • Verification: THREE exists in the file; the app still opens and works as before (SVG unchanged).
  WORKS OFFLINE: yes (the library is in the file).

STAGE 1 — 3D sandbox next to the SVG
  • Add a <canvas> next to the current positioning view. Scene: a perspective camera, lighting
    (ambient + directional), OrbitControls (rotate with finger/mouse). requestAnimationFrame render loop.
  • Primitives: sphere = head, capsule/cylinder = trunk, at [0,0,0]. Helper axes (AxesHelper).
  • Goal: confirm the axis mapping and that the canvas coexists with the rest of the UI.
  • Verification: Paweł sees a rotatable solid; I check there are no console/parser errors.

STAGE 2 — Quaternion bridge (math, verifiable without a preview)
  • A toThree(q) function per the principle above. Wire stepHeadQ → head3d.quaternion.
  • Numerical test (as before): for each position×face compare the nose/vertex/gravity direction
    in Three vs gHead. Error threshold as in K1 (~1e-3).
  • Visual verification (Paweł): the "blocky" head reacts correctly to the maneuver steps.

STAGE 3 — Procedural body rig
  • Build the Object3D hierarchy per SKEL (pelvis→spine→neck→head; limbs), offsets=position.
  • Poses (POSE3D) and orientations (composeHead/TORSO_Q) → bone.quaternion (via the Stage 2 bridge).
  • Numerical verification: the bones' world position in Three == bodyJoints (after the frame transform).
    Bone lengths preserved. Visual verification: the silhouette in every Semont/Epley position.

STAGE 4 — Labyrinth + otolith
  • Semicircular canals as THREE.CatmullRomCurve3 (the same paths as CANAL_PATHS, but in 3D).
  • Otolith = a small sphere; position = a point on the curve per phi from the engine (curve.getPointAt).
  • Semi-transparent skull/labyrinth (material transparent, opacity). Embed it in the head or beside it.
  • Verification: otolith motion consistent with the engine's phi(t) (numerically); Paweł judges legibility.

STAGE 5 — Smooth transitions (timeline)
  • Between steps: slerpQ (we have it) on the bones/head; the otolith along the curve. Optionally GSAP (it would also have to
    be inline for offline — or a custom tween on slerpQ, with no dependency).
  • Verification: the transitions reproduce the engine's interpolation.

STAGE 6 — Retire the SVG
  • Once the 3D stably reproduces all positions + nystagmus → remove the SVG silhouette render.
  • Optionally keep a 2D mode as an option/fallback (a toggle), if useful for teaching.
