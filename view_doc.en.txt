=== BÁRÁNY (ICVD) CLASSIFICATION + CENTRAL DIFFERENTIATION (CPN) — diagnostics ===
Card in renderDiag (svg-screens.js), below the mechanism card, above the treatment recommendation. Two views toggled by the
segment "Peripheral (BPPV) ↔ Central (CPN)" (state.diagCentral, handler toggleDiagCentral; full render).
 - PERIPHERAL (default): subtype label from pose/maneuvers.js:baranyClassify(canal,variant,side,antMode) —
   subtype + "established / emerging syndrome" (established/emerging) + feature chips (Latency / Duration /
   Fatigability / Direction / Affected side). Pure clinical function (like recommend/variantLabels) — a SYNTHESIS of conventions
   already encoded in DIAG (latNote/features), ZERO effect on physics. Golden captures this view (deterministic).
 - CENTRAL (after click): a .redflag block with the CPN red flags (no latency / persistent / non-fatigable /
   purely vertical — especially downbeat — or purely torsional / direction outside any canal / neutral position / neuro symptoms),
   a diagram of PERSISTENT DOWNBEAT (data-cpnnys: startNys from nysFromGeom("anterior",side,"cupulo",qSupineYaw(0)) with an
   OVERRIDDEN dir=0 → pure downbeat without torsion; persistent=true carries a persistent envelope), and swapping the
   "Suggested treatment" card for "Repositioning not indicated" (do not reposition → neuro + posterior-fossa MRI; mimic: vestibular
   migraine). Reset diagCentral→false on openTest/pickTest (fresh entry into a test). Criteria: otoneurology
   memory "BPPV — Bárány 2015 criteria".

=== OTOLITHIC PANEL — SVV & VEMP (HINTS) ===
A panelbox in renderHints (after "Skew deviation"), visible in EVERY mode (scenario + custom). It visualizes
the READY outputs of the NeuroVOR.svv(p) and NeuroVOR.vemp(p) engine — no new physics. Static (no rAF) → refreshed by
swapping the innerHTML of the [data-otolith] container in refreshHintsCustom (saccule/utricle sliders + nerve selector).
 - SVV (svvSVG): a dark circle with a vertical reference line (true vertical, dashed) + a colored line tilted by
   svv.deg TOWARD the affected side (ipsiversively); convention as in the eye panels (R=screen-left, L=screen-right). Normal
   (deg<threshold) → vertical. Caption: "vertical tilt X° toward side …" + an OTR note.
 - VEMP: 4 horizontal amplitude bars (0..1) with a threshold marker (30%): cVEMP R/L (saccule → INFERIOR nerve, ipsilateral SCM) and
   oVEMP R/L (utricle → SUPERIOR nerve, contralateral inferior oblique m.). Color per status (normal/reduced/absent).
   A note localizes the weak side to a nerve branch. SCDS: a note that the model renders the third window through nystagmus, not amplitude.
 Patterns (verified): SUPERIOR nerve → oVEMP↓ + SVV↑ toward the affected side (cVEMP preserved); INFERIOR nerve → cVEMP↓ (oVEMP preserved).
 Visually completes the "end organ / nerve branch" map alongside vHIT (6 canals). [engine_doc: SVV/VEMP — H22]

=== DEBRIS SIZE/DENSITY — UI INTEGRATION (Repositioning) ===
The engine accepts `size` ("small"|"medium"|"big"; medium=r1=baseline) scaling the particle's dynamics
(tauP∝r⁻², gc∝r³, adh∝r — see engine_doc). The UI layer adds:
 - state.size (default "medium"). Selector in setup (Repositioning) and a compact `.sizepill` on the step screen
   (renderGuide, next to the step dots). pickSize(s): plan rebuild (new holds) + invalidation of the dynamics
   cache (key `name|side|size` in currentManSim) + re-render. Diagnostics NOT changed (medium default).
 - SIZE-DEPENDENT HOLDS (small = slow settling → longer position hold):
     • clinically recommended step hold: sizedSeconds() multiplies `st.seconds` (small ×1.6, 15 s grid;
       medium/big unchanged) in genPlan() — hence the slider/counter start from a longer value for small.
     • DYNAMICS hold (maneuverTimeline): hold cap 12 s → for small round(12/r²)≈20 s, so the slow debris
       has time to reach φ=178° (exit). Verified: small with longer holds CLEARS Epley/Semont/
       Yacovino/Lempert (without them Semont/Yacovino stalled at φ≈157°/128°). medium/big: cap 12 s (regression).
     • NOTE in the UI (small only): "the recommended position-hold time was extended" with a literature citation.
 - OTOLITH ANIMATION by size (setupGuideAnim): transition DUR ∝ 1/r² (Stokes settling velocity ∝ r²)
   → small ~2630 ms (slower), medium 1600 ms (unchanged), big ~878 ms (faster); clamp [800,3000].
   Nystagmus intensity/latency (startNys/startDialNys, manStepEnv envelope) follow directly from
   ξ(t) scaled by size — with no separate code.
 - Consistency note: sizeRadius() in the UI layer must stay CONSISTENT with SIZE_R in the Vestibular module.
 CITATION (rationale for longer holds for fine/slow-settling debris):
   Hain TC, Squires TM, Stone HA (2005). "Clinical implications of a mathematical model of BPPV."
   Ann N Y Acad Sci 1039:384-394. (rationale for holding the position ~30 s in CRP; slow settling of otoconia).
   Latency/strength variability by size: "Extremely Long Latency BPPV" (Res Vestib Sci, 2017).

=== CENTRAL↔PERIPHERAL DIFFERENTIATION — UI "HINTS" (engine module: NeuroVOR) ===
Third tab (alongside Repositioning and Diagnostics). state.screen==="hints"; renderHints() draws:
 - scenario selector (NeuroVOR.SCENARIOS) + VERDICT card (hintsVerdictHTML: peripheral/central verdict,
   HI·N·TS tags green/red, INFARCT block for central).
 - SPONTANEOUS NYSTAGMUS PANEL: eyesSVG (the same eyes as Diagnostics) + startNeuroNys — CONTINUOUS
   horizontal-torsional nystagmus (translate x = horizontal + rotate = torsion around the iris), amplitude ∝ strength from
   NeuroVOR.nystagmusAtGaze(p,gazeDeg,fixOn). Gaze deviation = translate x (camera mirror ~0.5 px/°).
   No tEnd (runs until the conditions change). Background ".dark" = Frenzel/darkness mode. Toggles: fixation
   (Frenzel/light) + gaze (L/center/R) → re-render.
 - HIT PANEL (not "vHIT" — V=video; the covert saccade is visible only in vHIT): hitSVG = FRONTAL VIEW of the face
   (head with ears + eyes + nose, target ○ above the head). PATIENT side markers (emk R/L) around the head, mirror:
   R=screen-left, L=screen-right. Buttons "Push toward the right ear (R)/left ear (L)" — PATIENT sides, not screen sides.
   startHIT(hi): timeline push→hold→corrective SACCADE→return. Head = translate x (yaw, illustrative); irises
   locally = (fixation_error − head_translate), eye-in-space = (1−gain)·amplitude; corr(t) cancels
   part of the undershoot: COVERT saccade during the push (T_COV..T_IMP), OVERT after (T_SAC). gain 1 → eyes on target.
 - SKEW PANEL: skewSVG (eyes R/L + cover) + startSkew: alternating movement of the cover; the uncovered eye
   makes a VERTICAL saccade dev→0; the covered eye drifts to misalignment (±skewDeg·4 px). No skew → eyes in line.
All loops via loopRAF (cleaned by cancelAnims on re-render). CSS: .tabs.three, .pillseg, .hint-eyes,
.hverdict.{peripheral|central|normal}, .hrow .tag.{ok|bad}. Color: peripheral=green (reassuring), central=red.

 Scene3D  (stage A)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
HEAD_POINTS — anatomical head points (unit, head frame):
   nose[0,0,1] top[0,1,0] chin[0,-1,0] earR[1,0,0] earL[-1,0,0]
   occiput[0,0,-1] eyeR[0.4,0.15,0.85] eyeL[-0.4,0.15,0.85]

CAMERAS — orthographic cameras {right, up, fwd} (world vectors):
   topDownBehind — above the head, from behind → REPRODUCES THE CURRENT DIAL (behind+swap)
   topDownFront  — above the head, from the front
   frontal       — facing the patient (eyes; MIRROR: the patient's right ear on the left of the screen)
   behind        — behind the head (the patient's right ear on the right of the screen)
   sideRight / sideLeft — from the side
   Convention: right = world→screen +x; up = world→"top of the screen";
               SVG y downward, so screen y = -(p·up); fwd = viewing direction (depth/occlusion).

project(pHead, qHead, cam) -> {x, y, depth}
   Rotates the head point by qHead (head→world) and projects through the camera.
   depth = p·fwd (for sorting/occlusion and the sign of chirality in the future).

screenAngleCW(p) -> degrees
   Clockwise angle from "top of the screen" — for schematic rotations.

=== figProj ARCHITECTURE: composeHead + 3D skeleton (FK) + projective renderer ===
(A design reference; figProj is today the ONLY silhouette renderer — see the SESSION SUMMARY below
 for the history of the transition from figSide/POSE and the list of fixed bugs.)

composeHead(body,face,yaw): head→world = BODY_Q[body] ∘ headPitchQ(body,face) ∘ qaxis([0,1,0],yaw)
  (body ∘ pitch ∘ neck twist). BODY_Q = head orientation at neutral face/yaw=0 (per body class).
  headPitchQ: pitch neutral→face determined from BASE_G via qFromToVec (the shortest rotation a→b).
  Special cases: supine*→Vestibular.qSupineYaw(yaw) (like stepHeadQ); leanL/R→qFromG(LEAN_G[...]).
  Core property: gHead(composeHead) == stepGravity for all combinations used (validated
  21/21, max err 0.00045) — the RENDER AND the ENGINE agree on the orientation, with the correct anatomical roll.

3D skeleton (FK): body frame x=right,y=up(skull),z=front(nose). SKEL = a tree [name,parent,offset] for
  the neutral posture (pelvis→spine→neck→head; spine→shoulders→elbows→hands; pelvis→hips→knees→ankles→toes).
  fkJoints(rot): forward kinematics, a joint rotation affects descendants via the worldRot of the parent chain.
  POSE3D[class]: articulation (joint angles, e.g. flexed hips in sit, flexed knees). TORSO_Q[body]: trunk
  orientation per body class. bodyJoints(body,face): FK(POSE3D)∘TORSO_Q → 3D joint positions (+ a dynamic neck,
  see the SESSION SUMMARY).

PoseSpec (Stage 2 of modularization, 2026-07-11): the canonical description of a step/phase pose — a SINGLE source for
  all renderers (SVG today, Three.js from Stage 3). Producer: pose/maneuvers.js →
  poseSpec({body,yaw,face}) = { body,yaw,face, headQ:composeHead(...), stepQ:stepHeadQ(...),
  gravity:stepGravity(...), joints:bodyJoints(...) }. The renderer does NOT derive the pose itself (closing the
  engine↔view divergence from the 2.5D audit): figProj reads spec.joints/spec.headQ, gravArrowFor reads
  spec.gravity, headDial reads spec.yaw/face. Debris physics (maneuverTimeline) uses stepQ; drawing
  the head headQ; invariant: gHead(headQ)==gravity==gHead(stepQ). Consumers: posture(spec,viewSide),
  figProj(spec,obsCam,opt), headDial(spec,headCamera,nys), gravArrowFor(spec). Camera/transitions
  DELIBERATELY outside the spec (view decisions, plan.headCamera) — Stage 3 will extend the spec when Three requires it.

figProj(spec,obsCam,opt): projection of the skeleton through the observer camera → {fig, headC}.
  - spec.joints → Scene3D.project(joint,I,obsCam) → {x,y,depth}; capsules sorted by depth (far→near).
  - HEAD: a circle at pos.head; face/profile/back from the projection of the nose+skull (spec.headQ), WITHOUT a separate faceAngle:
      |nose.depth|≤0.4 → profile (profileMarks); nose.depth<0 → face (frontFace); >0 → back; plus a screen-space
      rule "nose straight down → back" (see the Lempert STEP 5 section below) for side views.
  - opt.bedY: anchoring the WHOLE group to the couch (see the SESSION SUMMARY, the "floating patient" bug).
  - Sides (sideL/R): lying as supine + a roll around the long axis, so that the broadside faces the side camera
    (not a perspective foreshortening).
